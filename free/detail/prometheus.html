Let's Get Started

What is Prometheus?

Prometheus is like a super detective for computers, websites, and apps. It checks on them 24/7 to make sure they're working properly.

Imagine you have a video game console. Sometimes it might get too hot or run out of space. Prometheus would tell you about these problems before your game crashes!

Watching over your systems!

Prometheus in Simple Words

It Checks Vital Signs

Like a doctor checking your heartbeat, Prometheus checks how fast computers are working.

It Rings Alarms

When something goes wrong, Prometheus lets people know so they can fix it.

It Keeps Records

Prometheus remembers how things were working yesterday to spot problems today.

How Prometheus Works

1. It Collects Data

Prometheus goes out and gathers information (called metrics) from computers and apps, like how hot they are or how much work they're doing.

1

# Example of data Prometheus collects

computer_temperature = 35°C

website_response_time = 0.5 seconds

memory_used = 25%

# Prometheus checking every 15 seconds

scrape_interval: 15s

scrape_configs:

  - job_name: 'my_computer'

    targets: ['192.168.1.100:9090']

2

2. It Stores Information

Prometheus saves all the data it collects in its own database, so it can look back and see how things were working before.

3. It Checks for Problems

Prometheus uses special rules to know when something is wrong. For example, if a computer gets too hot, it creates an alert.

3

# Example rule: If temperature is too high

groups:

  - name: temperature_alerts

    rules:

      - alert: HighTemperature

        expr: computer_temperature > 70

# Alert notification

ALERT: HighTemperature

Severity: warning

Computer 192.168.1.100 is too hot!

Temperature: 75°C

4

4. It Sends Alerts

When Prometheus finds a problem, it sends messages to people who can fix it, like sending an alert to a computer technician.

Where is Prometheus Used?

Online Stores

Prometheus monitors shopping websites to make sure they work during busy sales, so people can buy things without problems.

Mobile Apps

App companies use Prometheus to make sure their apps load quickly and don't crash on your phone or tablet.

Game Servers

Game companies use Prometheus to keep their online games running smoothly, even when thousands of people play at once.

Cloud Services

Companies like Google, Amazon, and Microsoft use Prometheus to monitor their huge computer networks.
