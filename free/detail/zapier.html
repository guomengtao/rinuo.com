About Zapier

Zapier is a web-based automation tool that connects your favorite apps, such as Gmail, Slack, Mailchimp, and over 5,000 others.

What is Zapier?

Founded in 2011, Zapier allows users to create automated workflows (called "Zaps") between web applications to automate repetitive tasks without coding.

With Zapier, you can connect two or more apps to automate repetitive tasks, so you can focus on more important work. A Zap is made up of a trigger and one or more actions. When the trigger happens, Zapier automatically performs the actions for you.

Learn how Zapier works

Name Origin & Meaning

The name "Zapier" comes from the concept of "zapping" information between different applications. The founders wanted a name that conveyed speed and simplicity in connecting different services.

The "-ier" suffix was chosen to suggest a tool or service that makes a process easier, reflecting Zapier's mission to simplify automation for everyone.

Pros & Cons

An honest look at what makes Zapier stand out and where it could improve.

Advantages

  • Extensive library of 5,000+ app integrations
  • User-friendly interface requiring no coding knowledge
  • Reliable automation with good uptime record
  • Excellent documentation and customer support
  • Scalable plans for individuals and businesses of all sizes
  • Regular updates and new features

Limitations

  • Can become expensive for high-volume usage
  • Some advanced features require technical knowledge
  • Occasional delays in automation execution
  • Certain apps have limited functionality
  • Complex Zaps can be difficult to troubleshoot
  • Free plan has significant limitations on usage
