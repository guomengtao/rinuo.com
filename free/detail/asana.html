About Asana

Asana is a web and mobile work management platform designed to help teams organize, track, and manage their work. It's designed to eliminate the need for email communication about tasks and projects, centralizing all information in one place.

The platform allows teams to create projects, assign tasks, set deadlines, and communicate about work, all within a visual interface that helps keep everyone on the same page.

Today, Asana is used by millions of teams worldwide, from small businesses to large enterprises, across various industries.

Founders & Origin

The Story Behind Asana

Asana was founded in 2008 by Dustin Moskovitz and Justin Rosenstein, who previously worked together at Facebook. They recognized the inefficiencies in how teams collaborate and communicate about work, often relying on endless email threads and meetings.

The name "Asana" comes from a Sanskrit term referring to a yoga posture, reflecting the company's focus on finding balance and clarity in work. The company launched publicly in 2012 and has since grown to become one of the leading work management platforms globally.

Dustin Moskovitz

Co-founder

A computer programmer and entrepreneur, Dustin was one of the co-founders of Facebook. He left Facebook in 2008 to focus on Asana, where he serves as CEO.

Justin Rosenstein

Co-founder

A software engineer who previously worked at Google and Facebook, Justin is known for creating important features like Facebook's Like button. He co-created Asana to improve team productivity.

Features, Pros & Cons

Key Features

  • Task management with assignees and due dates
  • Project timelines and Gantt charts
  • Customizable dashboards and reports
  • Team collaboration tools and comments
  • Integration with 100+ third-party apps
  • Mobile apps for iOS and Android

Advantages

  • Intuitive and user-friendly interface
  • Powerful visualization of projects
  • Excellent for team collaboration
  • Robust API for custom integrations
  • Regular updates and new features
  • Comprehensive free plan available

Limitations

  • Advanced features require paid plans
  • Can feel overwhelming for new users
  • Mobile app has limited functionality
  • Reporting features could be more robust
  • No built-in time tracking
  • Steeper learning curve than simpler tools
