About Ghost

Ghost is a powerful app for professional publishers to create, share, and grow a business around their content.

Project Origin

Founded in 2013 as a Kickstarter project, Ghost emerged as a reaction to the complexity of existing blogging platforms. The goal was to create a simple, focused tool for professional writers.

The Founders

Ghost was created by John O'Nolan, previously lead developer of WordPress.com, and Hannah Wolfe. They assembled a team to build a platform that prioritizes content creation over complex features.

Mission

To create the best open source tools for independent publishers on the web. Ghost aims to make professional publishing accessible to anyone, regardless of technical ability.

The Technology Stack

Ghost is built with modern web technologies that prioritize performance, security, and developer experience.

  • Node.js backend with Express framework
  • Handlebars templating engine for themes
  • MySQL or SQLite database for content storage
  • RESTful API and GraphQL support
  • JavaScript-based admin interface

The Good and The Challenges

Like any platform, Ghost has its strengths and areas where it might not be the perfect fit.

Strengths

Content-First Design

Ghost puts writing at the center, with a clean editor that minimizes distractions and focuses on content creation.

Powerful Publishing Tools

Scheduling, previewing, and organizing content is intuitive and efficient, with built-in SEO tools.

Monetization Features

Built-in support for subscriptions, memberships, and premium content makes it easy to earn from your work.

Open Source Freedom

Self-host for free or use their managed service, with full control over your data and customization options.

Challenges

Learning Curve

While the editor is simple, setting up and customizing Ghost requires more technical knowledge than some platforms.

Theme Ecosystem

While growing, the theme marketplace isn't as extensive as competitors like WordPress.

Plugin Limitations

The plugin ecosystem is more limited compared to larger platforms, focusing on essential functionality.

Hosting Requirements

Self-hosting requires Node.js environment setup, which can be challenging for non-technical users.
