The Story Behind Freenom

John founded Freenom in 2013 with a simple mission: to make the internet more accessible by providing free domain names to anyone with an idea worth sharing.

After working in the web hosting industry for over a decade, John noticed a significant barrier for many people looking to start online – the upfront cost of domain registration.

"I believed that access to the internet should be a right, not a privilege," says John. "Freenom was born from that belief, giving people worldwide the ability to establish their online presence for free."

Under John's leadership, Freenom has grown from a small startup to a global platform serving millions of users in over 200 countries.

The Pros and Cons

A balanced look at what makes Freenom great and where there's room for improvement.

The Pros

  • Free domain names with multiple extensions
  • No credit card required to get started
  • User-friendly interface and easy setup
  • Scalable options as your needs grow
  • Reliable uptime and performance
  • Strong security features included

The Cons

  • Free domains require annual renewal
  • Limited support options for free accounts
  • Some extensions may be restricted in certain countries
  • Premium features require paid plans
  • Domain transfer process can be complex
  • Occasional delays in verification for new accounts
