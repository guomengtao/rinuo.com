About Unsplash

Unsplash is a website dedicated to sharing stock photography under the Unsplash License. It has become one of the largest and most popular sources of free high-resolution images on the internet.

What started as a simple side project has evolved into a global community of photographers and creators, all contributing to a shared resource of stunning imagery.

The Founder

Mikael Cho

Mikael Cho is the founder of Unsplash, which he started in 2013 as a side project while running his design agency, Crew.

The idea came about when he was working on a client project and struggled to find high-quality, free images. He asked a photographer friend to share 10 photos, and Unsplash was born.

Under his leadership, Unsplash grew from a simple blog post with 10 photos to a global platform with millions of images and contributors worldwide.

The Origin Story

Unsplash began in 2013 when founder Mikael Cho was working on a project for his design agency and couldn't find high-quality, free images to use.

He reached out to a photographer friend, Luke Chesser, who agreed to share 10 of his photos for free use. Cho posted these photos on his blog with a simple idea: "high-resolution photos, free for personal and commercial use."

The response was overwhelming. The post went viral, and people started requesting more photos. What started as a side project quickly grew into something much bigger.

In 2017, Unsplash became a standalone company, and in 2021, it was acquired by Getty Images, while maintaining its commitment to providing free, high-quality imagery.

Technology Stack

The Tech Behind Unsplash

  • Frontend: React, JavaScript, CSS/SCSS
  • Backend: Ruby on Rails, Node.js
  • Database: PostgreSQL, Redis
  • Image Processing: Custom solutions built on libvips
  • Search: Elasticsearch for powerful image discovery
  • Infrastructure: AWS, Docker, Kubernetes
  • APIs: RESTful API with GraphQL support

Pros & Cons

Advantages

  • High-quality, professional-grade photography

  • Generous license allowing commercial use without attribution

  • Vast library with millions of images across diverse categories

  • Powerful search functionality with advanced filters

  • Well-documented API for developers

  • Regularly updated with new content

Disadvantages

  • Some popular niches may have limited options

  • High resolution downloads require attribution in some cases

  • API has rate limits for free usage

  • Some images may appear generic or overused

  • Quality can vary between contributors

  • Advanced features require a Pro subscription

How to Use Unsplash

1. Discover

Browse through categories or use the search bar to find images that match your needs. Use filters to refine results by orientation, color, and more.

2. Download

Click on any image to view it in full size, then select your preferred resolution and download it to your device.

3. Use

Use the images for personal or commercial projects. While attribution isn't required, it's always appreciated by photographers.

License Information

All photos on Unsplash are covered by the Unsplash License, which allows for:

  • Free use for both commercial and non-commercial purposes

  • No requirement for attribution (though appreciated)

  • Modification and adaptation of images

There are some restrictions, including using images to create a competing service or implying endorsement by the photographer or subject.
