The Story Behind Hoppscotch

Every great tool has an interesting origin story and a passionate creator.

Founder

Andrew Schmelyun

Andrew is a full-stack developer with a passion for creating tools that make developers' lives easier. He's known for his work on various open-source projects and his contributions to the web development community.

Project Origin

Hoppscotch was born out of Andrew's frustration with existing API testing tools that were either too slow, resource-heavy, or expensive. He wanted a lightweight alternative that could run directly in the browser without compromising on functionality.

What started as a personal project in 2019 quickly gained traction in the developer community, thanks to its focus on speed, simplicity, and user experience. Today, Hoppscotch is used by thousands of developers worldwide.

The Good and The Bad

No tool is perfect. Here's an honest look at Hoppscotch's strengths and weaknesses.

Advantages

  • Extremely fast and lightweight compared to alternatives
  • Completely free and open-source with no hidden costs
  • Simple, intuitive interface that's easy to learn
  • Works entirely in the browser with offline support
  • Active development and responsive community

Disadvantages

  • Less feature-rich than some enterprise alternatives
  • Limited documentation compared to more established tools
  • Some advanced features are still in development
  • Mobile experience, while functional, is not optimal
  • Limited enterprise support options
