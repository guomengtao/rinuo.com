About Zoom

Company Overview

Zoom Video Communications, Inc. is an American communications technology company headquartered in San Jose, California. The company provides cloud-based video conferencing services that allow users to conduct video meetings, voice calls, chat, and screen sharing.

Since its founding in 2011, Zoom has become one of the most popular video conferencing solutions worldwide, especially during the global COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 when the demand for remote work and online learning surged, significantly increasing Zoom's user base.

Name Meaning & Origin

The name "Zoom" comes from the word's meaning - to move quickly or suddenly approach, symbolizing the platform's ability to connect people quickly, bringing them "closer" despite geographical distances.

The company was founded in 2011 by Eric Yuan. Yuan, a former engineer at Cisco Webex, found that the video conferencing technology at the time wasn't user-friendly enough, so he decided to create a simpler, more reliable solution. Zoom launched its first product version in 2013 and successfully went public in 2019.

Founder Information

Eric Yuan

Born in Shandong, China, Eric Yuan immigrated to the United States in 1997. Before founding Zoom, he worked at Cisco for 14 years, eventually serving as Vice President of Engineering, overseeing Cisco's collaboration software product line, including WebEx.

Yuan left Cisco in 2011 to found Zoom. His vision was to create a platform that would allow people to connect easily, no matter where they were located. Under his leadership, Zoom has grown into a multi-billion dollar company with millions of users worldwide.

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Excellent video and audio quality that remains stable even with poor network conditions

  • Intuitive interface that's easy to use with almost no learning curve for new users

  • Strong cross-platform compatibility supporting nearly all major operating systems and devices

  • Feature-rich with useful tools like screen sharing, virtual backgrounds, and meeting recording

  • Offers a free plan that meets basic needs for individuals and small teams

  • Integrates well with various calendar and collaboration tools

Cons

  • Free plan has a 40-minute time limit on meetings with 3+ participants

  • Advanced features require paid plans, making long-term use potentially costly

  • Has had some security and privacy issues in the past, though significantly improved

  • May experience lag or quality degradation with limited network bandwidth

  • Some advanced features (like large webinars) have a steeper learning curve

  • Access may be restricted in certain regions

Industry Comparison

How Zoom stacks up against other leading video conferencing tools across key features

Zoom

Best overall performance with excellent video quality and ease of use, suitable for teams of all sizes. Free plan has limitations but paid options are flexible.

Microsoft Teams

Tightly integrated with Microsoft 365 with powerful collaboration features, ideal for enterprises already using the Microsoft ecosystem. Good video quality but more complex interface.

Google Meet

Seamlessly integrates with Google Workspace and is easy to use, perfect for small teams and educational institutions. Free version has limitations but pricing is affordable.
