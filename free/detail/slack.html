Origin Story & Founders

From a gaming company's internal tool to a global communication platform.

The Birth of Slack

Slack was born in 2013 from the internal communication tool used by Tiny Speck, a Vancouver-based gaming company founded by Stewart Butterfield.

The company was developing an online game called Glitch, which ultimately failed, but their internal messaging system proved so useful that they decided to pivot and develop it into a standalone product.

Slack launched publicly in February 2014 and quickly gained traction, reaching 10,000 daily active users within 24 hours. The name is an acronym for "Searchable Log of All Conversation and Knowledge."

Stewart Butterfield

Co-founder & CEO

Stewart is a serial entrepreneur who previously co-founded Flickr, one of the first popular photo-sharing platforms, which was acquired by Yahoo in 2005.

He led Slack from its inception through its acquisition by Salesforce in 2021 for $27.7 billion, one of the largest software acquisitions in history.

Cal Henderson

Co-founder & CTO

Cal is a British software engineer who also co-founded Flickr with Stewart Butterfield. He serves as Slack's CTO, overseeing the technical development and infrastructure.

He's known for his expertise in scalable web architectures and has written several books on web development and engineering management.

Pros & Cons

An honest look at what makes Slack shine and where it could improve.

Advantages

  • Intuitive interface that's easy to learn and use

  • Extensive integration ecosystem with thousands of apps

  • Powerful search functionality across all content

  • Excellent mobile experience with feature parity

  • Robust security features suitable for enterprise use

  • Effective organization of conversations through channels

Disadvantages

  • Premium plans can be expensive for larger teams

  • Free plan has limitations on message history and integrations

  • Can become noisy with too many active channels

  • Steeper learning curve for advanced features

  • Occasional performance issues with large workspaces

  • Limited customization options for the interface
