What is Trello?

Trello is a web-based, Kanban-style, list-making application designed to help teams organize and prioritize projects in a flexible and visual way.

A Visual Workspace

Trello's boards, lists, and cards enable teams to organize and prioritize projects in a fun, flexible, and rewarding way. Visualize your workflow at a glance, see what's being worked on, who's working on what, and where something is in a process.

From sales and marketing to HR and software development, Trello helps teams move work forward and stay organized.

Project Origin

Trello emerged from Fog Creek Software, a company founded by Joel Spolsky and Michael Pryor in 2000. The idea for Trello was born out of the team's frustration with existing project management tools that were either too complex or not visual enough.

Development began in 2010 when Daniel Gross joined Fog Creek and proposed a simpler, more visual approach to task management inspired by Kanban boards. The first version of Trello was released to the public in 2011.

In 2017, Trello was acquired by Atlassian, the Australian software company known for products like Jira and Confluence, allowing it to continue growing while maintaining its unique approach to project management.

Pros & Cons

A balanced look at Trello's advantages and limitations.

Advantages

  • Simple and intuitive interface that's easy to learn
  • Highly visual approach makes project status clear
  • Flexible for different types of projects and workflows
  • Excellent mobile apps for on-the-go access
  • Robust API and hundreds of integrations
  • Free plan available with core functionality

Limitations

  • Can become unwieldy for very complex projects
  • Advanced features require paid plans
  • Reporting and analytics are limited compared to specialized tools
  • Can experience performance issues with very large boards
  • Limited customization options for workflows
  • Offline functionality is limited
