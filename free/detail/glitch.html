About Glitch

The story behind the platform that's revolutionizing how developers collaborate and create.

Our Origins

Glitch was born in 2016 from a simple idea: building websites should be as easy as editing a document. Our founding team was frustrated by the complexity of traditional web development workflows and wanted to create something more accessible.

What started as a small side project quickly grew into a community of millions, all united by the desire to build and share creative web projects without barriers.

Today, Glitch remains committed to its open-source roots while continuing to innovate and provide powerful tools for developers of all skill levels.

"Coding should be fun, collaborative, and accessible to everyone."

Pros & Cons

An honest look at what makes Glitch shine and where it continues to improve.

Advantages

  • Instant Deployment

    Your code goes live as soon as you start typing, no complicated setup required.

  • Real-time Collaboration

    Work with teammates simultaneously with Google Docs-style editing.

  • Generous Free Tier

    Build and host full applications at no cost with reasonable resource limits.

  • Vibrant Community

    Millions of developers sharing projects, code snippets, and offering help.

  • Beginner Friendly

    Lower barrier to entry with intuitive interface and helpful documentation.

Limitations

  • Resource Constraints

    Free tier has limitations on memory, storage, and execution time.

  • Cold Starts

    Apps may experience delays when accessed after periods of inactivity.

  • Custom Domain Limits

    Free users can't connect custom domains to their projects.

  • Learning Curve

    Advanced features require understanding Glitch-specific workflows.

  • Privacy Concerns

    Public projects are visible to the entire community by default.
