What is Figma?

Figma is a cloud-based design and prototyping tool that's taking the design world by storm. Unlike traditional design software that lives on your computer, Figma lives in the browser, making it accessible from anywhere with an internet connection.

It's built around real-time collaboration, letting designers work together on the same file simultaneously â€“ seeing each other's cursors and edits in real time. This revolutionary approach has transformed how design teams collaborate, eliminating version control issues and streamlining the feedback process.

From wireframing to high-fidelity designs, prototyping to developer handoff, Figma covers the entire design process in one cohesive platform.

Why Designers Love Figma

Advantages

  • Cloud-based access from anywhere on any device
  • Real-time multiplayer collaboration
  • Powerful prototyping with interactive hotspots
  • Developer-friendly with inspect mode and CSS exports
  • Extensive plugin ecosystem for added functionality
  • Version history and branching for design exploration

Disadvantages

  • Requires stable internet connection for full functionality
  • Can be slower than native apps for very complex files
  • Offline mode has limited capabilities
  • Advanced features require a paid subscription
  • Steeper learning curve for designers used to Adobe products

The Story Behind Figma

Project Origin

Figma was founded in 2012 after Dylan Field received a Thiel Fellowship, which provided $100,000 to drop out of Brown University and pursue his startup idea.

The initial concept aimed to solve the collaboration problems Field observed in design tools. After four years of development, Figma launched its public beta in 2016, introducing the world to browser-based, real-time collaborative design.

Dylan Field

CEO & Co-founder

Dylan Field is the CEO and co-founder of Figma. Before starting Figma, he studied computer science at Brown University and was a Thiel Fellow. His vision for Figma was to create a design tool that would make collaboration seamless.

Dylan C. Robinson

Co-founder

Dylan C. Robinson co-founded Figma with Dylan Field. With a background in software engineering, Robinson played a crucial role in developing Figma's core technology, including its vector graphics renderer and real-time collaboration features.
