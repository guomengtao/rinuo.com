Name Meaning & Origin

The Story Behind the Name

"LeetCode" gets its name from "Leet" (also written as "1337"), a hacker slang term describing a way of writing using numbers and symbols instead of letters, representing elite computer skills. "Code" obviously refers to programming code.

Together, "LeetCode" symbolizes a platform that helps developers level up their coding skills to "leet" status.

Platform Origins

LeetCode was founded in 2015 by former Google engineer Herman Xu, with headquarters in Santa Clara, California.

The idea came from solving pain points in technical interview preparation, creating a centralized platform to help programmers practice algorithms and data structures to ace interviews at top tech companies.

Founder Information

Herman Xu

Founder & CEO of LeetCode

Herman graduated from Shanghai Jiao Tong University before earning a master's degree in computer science from Stanford University. Prior to founding LeetCode, he worked as a software engineer at Google, gaining valuable technical and interview experience.

Drawing on his deep understanding of technical interview processes, Herman created LeetCode to help programmers worldwide improve their algorithm skills and better tackle interview challenges.

How to Use for Free

  1. 1

    Create an Account

    Visit the LeetCode website and sign up for free with your email or GitHub account - no credit card required.

  2. 2

    Choose Problems

    Browse the problem bank, filter by difficulty, topic, or company tags to find suitable problems to practice.

  3. 3

    Write and Submit Code

    Write your code in the online editor and submit - the system will automatically judge and provide feedback.

  4. 4

    Join the Community

    Check out other users' solutions in the discussion forums or share your own approach.

  5. 5

    Participate in Contests

    Join weekly free contests to improve your problem-solving speed and skills while competing with users worldwide.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • High-quality problems with comprehensive coverage, closely matching real interview questions

  • Supports multiple programming languages including Python, Java, C++, and other mainstream languages

  • Active community with abundant solution approaches and discussions

  • Detailed problem categorization and learning paths for systematic study

  • Regular coding contests to enhance practical skills

  • Clean interface with good user experience and multi-platform synchronization

Cons

  • Advanced features require paid subscription, which is relatively expensive

  • Some problem explanations lack detail and aren't beginner-friendly enough

  • Overemphasis on algorithms and data structures, limited help for actual project development skills

  • Some users report occasional instability in the judging system

  • Limited access to problems for free users

  • Community contains some low-quality content and unfriendly discussions

Industry Comparison

Platform Capability Radar Chart

Main Competitors

HackerRank

Strengths: More corporate partnerships, better for job opportunities; richer project-based problems.

Weaknesses: Less depth in algorithm problems than LeetCode; lower community activity.

CodeSignal

Strengths: More scientific assessment system; better for simulating real interview environments.

Weaknesses: Fewer problems; limited free content.

Codewars

Strengths: Good gamified learning experience; lively community atmosphere.

Weaknesses: Problems less relevant to actual interviews; lacks systematic learning paths.

HackerEarth

Strengths: More open-source projects; abundant job opportunities for the Indian market.

Weaknesses: Less international; platform features less comprehensive than LeetCode.
